# Job Description Analysis Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should analyze job descriptions and help students tailor resumes, LinkedIn profiles, interview preparation, and learning plans.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Understand what a role is really asking for.
- Extract required and preferred skills.
- Identify hidden expectations.
- Compare the role with the student's profile.
- Recommend resume and LinkedIn tailoring.
- Create interview preparation priorities.
- Identify skill gaps and learning actions.

## JD Analysis Philosophy

A job description is a signal document. It contains explicit requirements and implicit priorities.

CareerOS should teach students to read beyond the words and infer:

- What problems the role solves
- what success looks like
- what evidence recruiters will look for
- which skills matter most
- what gaps need action

## Analysis Framework: ROLE

- Requirements: skills, education, experience, tools
- Outcomes: what the person is expected to deliver
- Language: keywords, competencies, repeated themes
- Evidence: proof the student should show

## JD Breakdown Sections

### Role Summary

Explain the role in plain language:

- What the role does
- who it supports
- what business problem it solves
- what a good candidate likely demonstrates

### Required Skills

Separate:

- Must-have skills
- preferred skills
- transferable skills
- tools
- certifications
- behavioral competencies

### Hidden Expectations

Infer from language:

- "Fast-paced" may indicate ambiguity and pressure.
- "Stakeholder management" indicates communication and coordination.
- "Data-driven" indicates comfort with metrics and analysis.
- "Cross-functional" indicates collaboration outside one team.
- "Ownership" indicates independence and accountability.

### Keyword Map

Group keywords by:

- Functional skills
- tools
- industry knowledge
- soft skills
- outcomes

Use naturally in resume and LinkedIn. Do not stuff.

### Candidate Fit

Compare student profile:

- Strong matches
- partial matches
- missing evidence
- skill gaps
- positioning risks

### Resume Tailoring

Recommend:

- Which bullets to move higher
- which skills to emphasize
- which projects to include
- which keywords to add naturally
- what evidence to strengthen

### Interview Preparation

Convert JD into likely interview questions:

- Motivation questions
- competency questions
- role-specific questions
- scenario questions
- weakness or gap questions

## Fit Score

Use a simple fit score when helpful:

- 80 to 100: strong fit, focus on tailored positioning.
- 60 to 79: viable fit, address gaps and strengthen evidence.
- 40 to 59: stretch role, build proof and consider adjacent roles.
- Below 40: likely mismatch unless student has missing evidence not shown.

Never use score as a final judgment. Use it as a diagnostic.

## Common Mistakes

- Applying without understanding the role.
- Copying keywords without evidence.
- Treating preferred qualifications as mandatory.
- Ignoring repeated themes.
- Not preparing for obvious gap questions.
- Using the same resume bullets for different roles.

## JD Analysis Checklist

- Is the role objective clear?
- Are must-have skills identified?
- Are repeated keywords captured?
- Are hidden expectations inferred?
- Are evidence gaps identified?
- Are resume tailoring actions clear?
- Are interview questions generated?
- Are learning priorities defined?

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- JD summary
- keyword map
- fit analysis
- resume tailoring checklist
- LinkedIn keyword recommendations
- interview questions
- skill gap roadmap
- application decision guidance

## Decision Tree

1. Did the student provide a JD?
   - Yes: analyze directly.
   - No: ask for JD or role title.
2. Does the student have a resume?
   - Yes: compare JD to resume.
   - No: provide JD requirements and ask for profile.
3. Is the student applying soon?
   - Yes: prioritize resume tailoring and interview prep.
   - No: include learning roadmap and evidence-building.
