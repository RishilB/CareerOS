# Networking Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should help students build professional relationships with alumni, recruiters, mentors, peers, faculty, and industry professionals.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Network with clarity and professionalism.
- Write thoughtful outreach messages.
- Prepare for informational conversations.
- Follow up well.
- Build relationships without sounding transactional.
- Use networking for learning, referrals, and opportunity discovery.

## Networking Philosophy

Networking is not asking strangers for jobs. It is building professional context and trust over time.

Good networking should be:

- Specific
- respectful
- concise
- relevant
- mutually aware
- consistent

## Networking Strategy Framework

Use the MAP framework:

- Motive: Why are you reaching out?
- Audience: Who is the right person?
- Path: What is the next realistic ask?

## Networking Audiences

### Alumni

Best for:

- Role discovery
- company insight
- placement advice
- referrals after relationship is built

### Recruiters

Best for:

- role clarification
- application process
- professional visibility

### Industry Professionals

Best for:

- domain insight
- skill expectations
- career path understanding

### Faculty and Mentors

Best for:

- direction
- feedback
- credibility
- introductions

### Peers

Best for:

- shared preparation
- accountability
- interview practice
- resource exchange

## Outreach Message Structure

Use:

1. Context
2. Specific reason for reaching out
3. Small request
4. Respectful close

Example:

"Hi [Name], I am an MBA student specializing in marketing and exploring brand strategy roles. I saw your work in consumer insights at [Company] and wanted to learn how you built your path after MBA. Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation next week? I would value your guidance."

## Follow-Up Structure

After a conversation:

1. Thank them.
2. Mention one specific insight.
3. State one action you will take.
4. Keep the relationship open.

## Informational Interview Questions

Good questions:

- How did you decide this role was right for you?
- What skills matter most in your current work?
- What do MBA students misunderstand about this function?
- What would you recommend I build before applying?
- What makes candidates stand out?

Avoid:

- "Can you get me a job?"
- "Can you refer me?" as the first message.
- Questions easily answered online.

## Referral Etiquette

Ask for referrals only when:

- There is a specific role.
- The person has context about you.
- Your resume is ready.
- You can explain fit clearly.
- The relationship is not purely transactional.

Referral ask structure:

"I found a role that aligns strongly with my background in [evidence]. If you feel comfortable after reviewing my resume, would you be open to referring me or advising me on the best application path?"

## Common Mistakes

- Sending generic messages.
- Asking for jobs too early.
- Making messages too long.
- Not researching the person.
- Not following up.
- Not tracking conversations.
- Treating networking as a one-time activity.

## Networking Checklist

- Is the target audience clear?
- Is the message personalized?
- Is the ask small and specific?
- Is the profile ready before outreach?
- Is the follow-up prepared?
- Is there a tracker?
- Is the tone professional?

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- Alumni outreach message
- recruiter message
- follow-up note
- informational interview question list
- networking strategy
- referral request
- relationship tracker
- weekly networking plan

## Decision Tree

1. Does the student know who they want to contact?
   - Yes: draft targeted message.
   - No: define audience and search strategy.
2. Is the student asking for a referral?
   - Yes: check readiness and relationship context.
   - No: create learning-focused outreach.
3. Is the profile ready?
   - Yes: proceed.
   - No: improve LinkedIn and resume first.
