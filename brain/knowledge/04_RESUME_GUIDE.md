# Resume Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should help MBA students build, review, and improve resumes for internships, placements, and early-career roles.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Build role-aligned resumes.
- Improve recruiter readability.
- Strengthen achievement statements.
- Optimize for ATS without keyword stuffing.
- Translate academic, internship, project, and leadership work into credible evidence.
- Avoid exaggeration, vague claims, and misleading accomplishments.

## Resume Philosophy

A resume is not a biography. It is a targeted evidence document.

Every line should help a recruiter answer:

- What can this candidate do?
- Where is the proof?
- Is this relevant to the role?
- Can I understand the value in seconds?

## MBA Resume Principles

Strong MBA resumes are:

- Targeted to a role family.
- Evidence-led.
- Concise.
- Outcome-oriented.
- Easy to scan.
- Honest.
- Consistent in formatting.
- Balanced across education, experience, leadership, skills, and projects.

## Recommended Resume Structure

### Header

Include:

- Name
- Phone
- Email
- LinkedIn
- Portfolio or GitHub if relevant
- City or location if useful

Avoid:

- Full address
- Unprofessional email
- Excessive links
- Personal details not required for hiring

### Summary

For most MBA students, a summary is optional. Use it only if it sharpens positioning.

Good summary:

"MBA marketing candidate with experience in consumer research, campaign planning, and event leadership; seeking brand strategy and digital growth roles."

Weak summary:

"Hardworking and passionate MBA student looking for an opportunity to learn and grow."

### Education

Include:

- Institution
- Degree
- Specialization
- Graduation year
- CGPA if helpful
- Relevant coursework
- Honors, if real

### Experience

Include internships, work experience, live projects, consulting projects, or significant roles.

Each bullet should show:

- Action
- Scope
- Method
- Result or learning
- Relevance to target role

### Projects

Projects matter when full-time experience is limited.

Include:

- Business problem
- Methodology
- Tools
- Analysis
- Recommendation
- Outcome or deliverable

### Leadership and Activities

Use when leadership evidence strengthens the target profile.

Include:

- Team size
- Responsibility
- event scale
- stakeholders
- outcomes

### Skills

Group skills by category.

Examples:

- Business: market research, financial analysis, stakeholder management
- Tools: Excel, Power BI, Tableau, Canva, SQL
- Communication: presentations, report writing, interviewing

Avoid inflated skill lists.

## ATS Principles

ATS optimization means clarity and relevance, not tricking software.

Use:

- Standard section headings
- Text-based formatting
- Clear role keywords
- Full forms and common abbreviations where useful
- Consistent date formats
- Simple bullets

Avoid:

- Tables if they break parsing
- Icons as labels
- Keyword stuffing
- Hidden text
- Decorative columns that harm readability

## Achievement Writing Framework

Use the AIM framework:

- Action: What did the student do?
- Impact: What changed or improved?
- Measurement: What evidence supports it?

If exact metrics are unavailable, use credible scope:

- number of team members
- number of respondents
- size of event
- number of stakeholders
- duration
- deliverable created
- process improved

## STAR Bullet Method

Convert raw work into resume bullets using:

- Situation: context
- Task: responsibility
- Action: what was done
- Result: outcome

Resume bullet format:

"Action verb + task/scope + method/tool + result/relevance."

Example:

Raw:

"Worked on a market research project."

Improved:

"Conducted consumer research with 60 survey responses to identify purchase drivers and presented 3 positioning recommendations for a new FMCG product concept."

## Action Verb Categories

### Analysis

Analyzed, evaluated, assessed, benchmarked, researched, diagnosed, modeled, interpreted.

### Strategy

Developed, designed, formulated, recommended, prioritized, mapped, structured.

### Execution

Led, coordinated, implemented, managed, organized, delivered, launched.

### Communication

Presented, synthesized, briefed, drafted, facilitated, negotiated, communicated.

### Improvement

Improved, streamlined, reduced, increased, accelerated, optimized, strengthened.

## Recruiter Expectations

Recruiters look for:

- Role fit
- Relevant evidence
- Clear communication
- Progression
- Achievement orientation
- Credibility
- Professional polish
- No obvious exaggeration

They often scan quickly. The strongest evidence should appear early.

## Resume Review Methodology

CareerOS should review resumes in this order:

1. Target alignment
2. Section structure
3. Evidence quality
4. Bullet strength
5. ATS readability
6. Formatting consistency
7. Skills relevance
8. Risk of overclaiming
9. Missing assets
10. Priority fixes

## Resume Scoring Areas

Use the resume rubric for scoring, but diagnose across:

- Target clarity
- Evidence strength
- Achievement quality
- Relevance
- Readability
- ATS compatibility
- Professional tone
- Formatting
- Differentiation

## Common Mistakes

- Using one resume for every role.
- Listing responsibilities without outcomes.
- Using generic adjectives instead of evidence.
- Adding too many tools without proof.
- Inflating achievements.
- Writing bullets that are too long.
- Hiding strong projects under weak sections.
- Ignoring target job descriptions.
- Making the resume visually decorative but hard to scan.

## Resume Checklist

Before finalizing:

- Is the target role clear?
- Does the top half show the strongest evidence?
- Does each bullet start with a strong action verb?
- Are bullets specific and credible?
- Are real metrics or scope included where possible?
- Are keywords naturally aligned to the role?
- Is formatting consistent?
- Are dates and titles clear?
- Is the resume free of grammar errors?
- Does the resume avoid fake or exaggerated claims?

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- Resume diagnosis
- Recruiter-style review
- ATS optimization recommendations
- Rewritten bullets
- Section restructuring
- Targeted resume strategy
- Skills section cleanup
- Resume score
- Priority improvement checklist

## Decision Tree

1. Does the student have a target role or JD?
   - Yes: tailor review to that role.
   - No: ask for target; if unavailable, review for general MBA employability.
2. Does the resume contain real achievements?
   - Yes: sharpen bullets.
   - No: identify evidence sources and ask follow-up questions.
3. Is the resume too generic?
   - Yes: reposition around target role.
   - No: improve clarity and proof.
4. Is the student applying soon?
   - Yes: prioritize top 5 fixes.
   - No: provide deeper restructuring.
