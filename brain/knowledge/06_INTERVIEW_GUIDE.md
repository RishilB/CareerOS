# Interview Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should prepare students for HR, behavioral, technical, case-style, and company-specific interviews.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Understand likely interview expectations.
- Build structured answers.
- Practice with realistic follow-up questions.
- Improve confidence through preparation.
- Connect resume evidence to role requirements.
- Communicate clearly under pressure.

## Interview Philosophy

An interview is not a memory test. It is a live evaluation of fit, judgment, communication, evidence, and self-awareness.

CareerOS should help students prepare answers that are:

- Structured
- truthful
- relevant
- concise
- evidence-backed
- reflective

## Interview Types

### HR Interview

Focus:

- Motivation
- fit
- communication
- attitude
- self-awareness
- stability
- salary expectations
- culture alignment

Common questions:

- Tell me about yourself.
- Why this role?
- Why this company?
- What are your strengths and weaknesses?
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
- Why should we hire you?

### Behavioral Interview

Focus:

- Past behavior as evidence of future behavior.
- Leadership
- teamwork
- conflict
- failure
- ownership
- problem-solving

Use STAR, but keep answers natural.

### Technical or Functional Interview

Focus depends on role:

- Marketing: consumer insights, campaigns, segmentation, metrics
- Finance: accounting, valuation, markets, financial analysis
- HR: talent, employee engagement, organization behavior
- Operations: process, supply chain, efficiency, systems
- Analytics: data interpretation, dashboards, SQL, business insight
- Consulting: structured problem solving, estimation, cases, business judgment

### Case Interview

Focus:

- Problem structuring
- hypothesis thinking
- quantitative reasoning
- communication
- synthesis

### Stress or Pressure Interview

Focus:

- composure
- clarity
- honesty
- recovery

CareerOS should teach students to pause, structure, and respond without defensiveness.

## Answer Frameworks

### STAR

- Situation: context
- Task: responsibility
- Action: what you did
- Result: outcome

Add Reflection when appropriate:

- What you learned
- How you would apply it to the target role

### CAR

- Context
- Action
- Result

Useful for shorter answers.

### PREP

- Point
- Reason
- Example
- Point

Useful for opinions, group discussions, and conceptual answers.

### CareerOS Fit Answer

For "Why this role/company?", use:

1. Role attraction
2. Evidence of fit
3. Company-specific reason
4. Future contribution

## Story Bank

Every student should build 8 to 10 stories:

- Leadership
- teamwork
- conflict
- failure
- achievement
- analytical thinking
- communication
- initiative
- learning quickly
- ethical decision

Each story should include:

- Short title
- competency demonstrated
- STAR structure
- metrics or scope
- reflection
- target roles where useful

## Interview Preparation Workflow

1. Clarify role, company, interview type, date, and urgency.
2. Review resume and job description.
3. Identify likely competencies.
4. Build story bank.
5. Prepare company-specific answers.
6. Practice mock questions.
7. Give feedback on structure, evidence, clarity, and confidence.
8. Create final 24-hour preparation plan.

## Mock Interview Method

When running a mock:

- Ask one question at a time.
- Wait for the student's answer.
- Evaluate briefly.
- Score if useful.
- Give one strength and one improvement.
- Ask a follow-up question.
- Repeat.

Do not answer for the student unless they ask for a model answer.

## Confidence Coaching

Confidence comes from:

- Preparation
- story clarity
- repetition
- body language
- breathing
- recovery strategies
- familiarity with likely questions

Do not provide shallow motivation. Convert anxiety into preparation steps.

## Body Language Guidance

Offer general guidance:

- Sit upright.
- Maintain natural eye contact.
- Pause before answering.
- Keep hands calm.
- Listen fully.
- Avoid rushing.
- Smile naturally when appropriate.

Do not overemphasize body language at the expense of content.

## Common Mistakes

- Memorizing scripts.
- Giving answers with no evidence.
- Speaking too long.
- Using the same story for every question.
- Criticizing past employers or faculty.
- Overclaiming ownership.
- Not researching the company.
- Not preparing questions for the interviewer.
- Being unable to explain resume bullets.

## Interview Checklist

- Do you know the role requirements?
- Can you explain every resume bullet?
- Do you have 8 to 10 STAR stories?
- Can you answer "Tell me about yourself" in 60 to 90 seconds?
- Can you explain why this company?
- Can you explain why this role?
- Do you have smart questions to ask?
- Have you practiced follow-ups?
- Have you prepared closing remarks?

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- Interview preparation plan
- Likely questions
- STAR story bank
- Model answers
- Mock interview
- Feedback scorecard
- Company-specific talking points
- Final 24-hour plan

## Decision Tree

1. Is the interview within 48 hours?
   - Yes: triage likely questions, company research, resume defense, and story bank.
   - No: build deeper preparation plan.
2. Is the interview type known?
   - Yes: prepare accordingly.
   - No: prepare HR, behavioral, and role-specific basics.
3. Does the student have stories?
   - Yes: refine them.
   - No: extract stories from resume and experiences.
4. Does the student need practice?
   - Yes: run mock interview.
   - No: create written preparation brief.
