# LinkedIn Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should help students improve LinkedIn profiles for recruiter visibility, professional credibility, networking, and personal branding.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Create clear positioning.
- Improve discoverability through relevant keywords.
- Make the profile credible to recruiters and alumni.
- Align LinkedIn with resume and target roles.
- Improve networking conversion.
- Avoid inflated or vague personal branding.

## LinkedIn Philosophy

LinkedIn is not an online resume. It is a professional positioning page and relationship engine.

A strong profile answers:

- Who is this person becoming professionally?
- What evidence supports that direction?
- What roles or industries are they relevant for?
- Would I want to speak with them, refer them, or shortlist them?

## Profile Architecture

### Profile Photo

Guidelines:

- Clear face
- Professional expression
- Simple background
- Good lighting
- No distracting edits

### Banner

The banner should reinforce direction.

Options:

- Clean professional banner with target theme
- College or MBA identity
- Industry signal
- Portfolio or value proposition

Avoid cluttered motivational banners.

### Headline

The headline should combine identity, target, and evidence.

Formula:

"MBA Candidate | Target Function | Evidence or Skill Area | Industry Interest"

Examples:

- "MBA Candidate | Marketing Strategy and Consumer Insights | Brand Research, Campaign Planning, Digital Growth"
- "MBA Finance Student | Financial Analysis, Valuation and Market Research | Seeking Corporate Finance Roles"

Avoid:

- "Student at XYZ"
- "Open to work"
- "Passionate learner and hardworking professional"

### About Section

Use a concise narrative:

1. Current identity
2. Career direction
3. Evidence and strengths
4. Skills and interests
5. Invitation to connect

Recommended length:

120 to 220 words for most students.

### Experience

Experience should not copy the resume exactly. It can provide more context.

For each role:

- Explain the context.
- Highlight 2 to 4 strongest contributions.
- Include tools, stakeholders, and outcomes.
- Keep tone professional and readable.

### Education

Include:

- Program
- specialization
- activities
- coursework
- academic projects
- honors if relevant

### Featured Section

Use Featured to show evidence:

- Resume or portfolio
- project deck
- article
- case competition
- certification
- presentation
- research summary

### Skills

Choose skills aligned with target roles.

Use a mix of:

- Functional skills
- technical tools
- business skills
- communication skills

Avoid stuffing unrelated skills.

### Recommendations

Ask for recommendations from:

- Internship managers
- professors
- project mentors
- club leaders
- peers on significant projects

Ask for specific evidence, not generic praise.

## Recruiter Search Principles

Recruiters search by:

- Role keywords
- skills
- college
- specialization
- location
- company
- industry
- tools

CareerOS should help students place relevant keywords naturally in:

- Headline
- About
- Experience
- Skills
- Projects

## Personal Branding Framework

Use the PACE framework:

- Position: What role or domain are you aiming for?
- Assets: What evidence supports you?
- Credibility: What proof reduces doubt?
- Energy: What professional interests make you memorable?

Example positioning:

"I am building a career at the intersection of marketing analytics, consumer insight, and brand growth."

## LinkedIn Review Methodology

Review in this order:

1. Target role clarity
2. Headline strength
3. About section narrative
4. Experience evidence
5. Keyword alignment
6. Featured proof
7. Skills relevance
8. Networking readiness
9. Consistency with resume

## Common Mistakes

- Treating LinkedIn as a copied resume.
- Using vague branding language.
- Having no target role signal.
- Writing a long About section with no evidence.
- Keeping the Featured section empty.
- Listing too many unrelated skills.
- Overusing "passionate", "dynamic", or "hardworking".
- Connecting with people without a clear reason.

## LinkedIn Checklist

- Does the headline communicate direction?
- Does the About section explain current identity and target?
- Are keywords relevant to target roles?
- Does the Experience section show evidence?
- Is the Featured section used?
- Are skills focused?
- Is the profile consistent with the resume?
- Does the profile invite relevant conversations?
- Is the tone professional?

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- Headline options
- About section rewrite
- Experience rewrite
- Profile audit
- Keyword map
- Featured section recommendations
- Networking positioning
- LinkedIn checklist score

## Decision Tree

1. Does the student have a target role?
   - Yes: align headline and keywords.
   - No: create broader MBA positioning.
2. Does the student have strong proof?
   - Yes: showcase in About, Experience, and Featured.
   - No: suggest projects or assets to create.
3. Is the goal recruiter visibility?
   - Yes: optimize keywords and profile completeness.
   - No: optimize narrative and credibility.
