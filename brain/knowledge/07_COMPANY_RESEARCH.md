# Company Research Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how CareerOS should help students research companies for applications, interviews, networking, and career decisions.

## Objectives

CareerOS should help students:

- Understand a company quickly and accurately.
- Connect company context to role preparation.
- Prepare better interview answers.
- Ask informed questions.
- Identify business, culture, and role-fit signals.
- Avoid shallow or outdated company summaries.

## Research Philosophy

Company research should help the student speak with relevance, not recite facts.

A good company brief answers:

- What does the company do?
- How does it make money or create value?
- What is happening in its industry?
- What challenges or priorities might matter?
- Why is this role relevant?
- What should the student ask or emphasize?

## Company Research Framework

Use the CRAFT framework:

- Context: company overview, business model, industry, geography
- Relevance: role, function, and student fit
- Advantages: strengths, differentiators, market position
- Frictions: risks, challenges, competition, culture concerns
- Talking points: interview answers, questions, and resume alignment

## Research Components

### Company Overview

Include:

- What the company does
- Key products or services
- Customer segments
- geography
- size and scale, if known
- business units

### Industry Context

Include:

- Industry trends
- growth drivers
- regulations
- technology shifts
- customer behavior changes
- competitive pressure

### Business Model

Explain:

- How the company earns revenue
- who pays
- what value is delivered
- key cost drivers
- distribution model

### Competitors

Identify:

- Direct competitors
- substitutes
- emerging competitors
- differentiators

### SWOT

Use when helpful:

- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Opportunities
- Threats

Keep SWOT practical and role-relevant.

### Recent News

Use current, verifiable sources when available. If browsing is not available, ask the student to share recent links or state that current news should be verified.

Do not invent recent developments.

### Financials

For public companies, consider:

- Revenue direction
- profitability
- segments
- margins
- growth areas
- investor priorities

For private companies, use available public signals.

### Leadership and Culture

Include:

- Leadership priorities
- values
- culture signals
- employee reviews with caution
- leadership interviews
- annual reports
- company blogs

Avoid treating employer reviews as absolute truth.

## Interview Application

Turn research into:

- "Why this company" answer
- role-specific talking points
- smart questions
- possible interview themes
- resume alignment priorities
- follow-up note content

## Questions To Ask Recruiters or Interviewers

Good questions:

- "What priorities would this role be expected to support in the first 6 months?"
- "What capabilities make someone successful in this team?"
- "How does this function contribute to the company's current growth priorities?"
- "What problems is the team currently trying to solve?"

Avoid:

- Questions easily answered on the website.
- Salary questions too early unless context is appropriate.
- Questions that sound like the student has not researched.

## Common Mistakes

- Reading only the homepage.
- Memorizing facts without interpretation.
- Ignoring the role.
- Not connecting company priorities to personal fit.
- Using outdated news.
- Asking generic questions.
- Overpraising the company.

## Expected Outputs

CareerOS may produce:

- Company brief
- industry summary
- SWOT
- competitor map
- interview talking points
- role-specific research plan
- questions to ask interviewer
- resume alignment recommendations

## Decision Tree

1. Is the company name known?
   - Yes: build company brief.
   - No: ask for target company or industry.
2. Is the goal interview preparation?
   - Yes: include likely questions and talking points.
   - No: focus on career fit and research.
3. Is current information needed?
   - Yes: ask for links or use verified browsing when available.
   - No: provide stable research framework.
4. Is the target role known?
   - Yes: connect company research to role fit.
   - No: provide broader company understanding.
