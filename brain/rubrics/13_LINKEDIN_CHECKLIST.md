# LinkedIn Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist defines how CareerOS should evaluate LinkedIn profiles for MBA students and young professionals.

## Evaluation Method

Score out of 100 when useful.

| Area | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Positioning clarity | 20 |
| Headline | 15 |
| About section | 15 |
| Experience evidence | 15 |
| Keywords and discoverability | 10 |
| Featured and proof assets | 10 |
| Skills and endorsements | 5 |
| Professional polish | 10 |
| Total | 100 |

## Positioning Clarity: 20 Points

Strong profile:

- Makes career direction visible.
- Connects background to target roles.
- Shows a coherent professional narrative.

Weak profile:

- Reads like a student record only.
- Has no role signal.
- Tries to appeal to every field.

## Headline: 15 Points

Strong headline:

- Includes MBA identity.
- Signals target function or domain.
- Includes evidence or skill area.
- Avoids generic claims.

Weak headline:

- Only says "Student at..."
- Uses "open to work" without positioning.
- Contains vague adjectives.

## About Section: 15 Points

Strong About section:

- Explains current identity.
- States career direction.
- Includes evidence.
- Sounds human and professional.
- Ends with a relevant connection signal.

Weak About section:

- Too long.
- Too vague.
- No proof.
- Sounds AI-generated.

## Experience Evidence: 15 Points

Strong:

- Experience entries include context and contributions.
- Achievements are credible.
- Projects and leadership are positioned well.

Weak:

- Empty descriptions.
- Copied resume bullets without context.
- Vague responsibilities.

## Keywords and Discoverability: 10 Points

Strong:

- Relevant target keywords appear naturally.
- Tools and competencies match target roles.
- Profile is searchable.

Weak:

- No role-specific keywords.
- Keyword stuffing.
- Skills mismatch.

## Featured and Proof Assets: 10 Points

Strong:

- Includes portfolio evidence, projects, decks, articles, or certifications.
- Assets support target role.

Weak:

- Featured section is empty.
- Assets are irrelevant or low quality.

## Skills and Endorsements: 5 Points

Strong:

- Skills are focused.
- Top skills match target.

Weak:

- Random skill list.
- Missing obvious target skills.

## Professional Polish: 10 Points

Strong:

- Professional photo.
- clean banner.
- no grammar errors.
- consistent tone.
- resume alignment.

Weak:

- Unprofessional visuals.
- typos.
- inconsistent claims.

## Checklist

- Headline communicates role direction.
- About section has identity, target, evidence, and connection point.
- Experience entries show contribution.
- Keywords match target roles.
- Featured section contains proof.
- Skills are focused.
- Profile aligns with resume.
- Tone is professional and natural.
- Recruiter can understand value in under 30 seconds.

## Common Red Flags

- "Hardworking, passionate, dynamic" without evidence.
- No target role signal.
- Empty About section.
- Inconsistent timeline versus resume.
- Overly casual tone.
- No proof assets.
