# Placement Readiness Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric helps CareerOS evaluate whether a student is ready for placements, internships, or active job applications.

## Scoring Method

Score out of 100.

| Area | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Career direction | 15 |
| Resume readiness | 15 |
| LinkedIn readiness | 10 |
| Interview readiness | 20 |
| Company and role research | 10 |
| Skill fit | 10 |
| Networking activity | 10 |
| Execution system | 10 |
| Total | 100 |

## Career Direction: 15 Points

Strong:

- Has target roles and industries.
- Can explain why.
- Has decision criteria.

Weak:

- Applies randomly.
- No role clarity.
- Cannot explain fit.

## Resume Readiness: 15 Points

Strong:

- Resume is targeted.
- Evidence is strong.
- ATS compatible.

Weak:

- Generic resume.
- weak bullets.
- no tailoring process.

## LinkedIn Readiness: 10 Points

Strong:

- Clear headline.
- relevant About section.
- proof assets.

Weak:

- Incomplete profile.
- vague positioning.
- no recruiter visibility.

## Interview Readiness: 20 Points

Strong:

- Has story bank.
- can answer core questions.
- practices follow-ups.
- understands resume.

Weak:

- No STAR stories.
- vague answers.
- weak company motivation.

## Company and Role Research: 10 Points

Strong:

- Understands target companies.
- can discuss industry context.
- prepares smart questions.

Weak:

- Reads only job title.
- no company-specific preparation.

## Skill Fit: 10 Points

Strong:

- Has relevant skills.
- knows gaps.
- has learning plan.

Weak:

- Skills do not match roles.
- no plan to close gaps.

## Networking Activity: 10 Points

Strong:

- Has outreach plan.
- speaks with alumni or professionals.
- follows up well.

Weak:

- No networking.
- only applies cold.
- messages are generic.

## Execution System: 10 Points

Strong:

- Tracks applications.
- sets weekly priorities.
- reviews progress.

Weak:

- Reactive.
- misses deadlines.
- no follow-up habit.

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 85-100 | Placement-ready. Focus on targeted execution. |
| 70-84 | Close to ready. Fix priority gaps before high-value applications. |
| 55-69 | Partially ready. Needs structured preparation. |
| 40-54 | Not ready for competitive placement cycles. Build assets and practice. |
| Below 40 | Start with career direction, profile building, and fundamentals. |

## Common Readiness Risks

- No target role.
- Resume is generic.
- LinkedIn does not match resume.
- Cannot explain projects.
- No company research habit.
- No networking pipeline.
- Learning plan is disconnected from applications.
