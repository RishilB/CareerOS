# Resume Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric defines how CareerOS should evaluate MBA student resumes. It supports recruiter-style feedback, ATS readiness checks, and prioritized improvement plans.

## Scoring Method

Score the resume out of 100.

Use the score as a diagnostic, not as a guarantee of selection.

## Scoring Categories

| Category | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Target alignment | 15 |
| Evidence quality | 20 |
| Achievement writing | 15 |
| Recruiter readability | 15 |
| ATS compatibility | 10 |
| Skills relevance | 10 |
| Formatting and polish | 10 |
| Integrity and credibility | 5 |
| Total | 100 |

## 1. Target Alignment: 15 Points

Evaluates whether the resume is clearly aligned to a role, industry, or function.

Strong:

- Target direction is visible.
- Strongest experiences match target role.
- Keywords are naturally relevant.
- Irrelevant details are minimized.

Weak:

- Resume feels generic.
- No clear role direction.
- Important target evidence is buried.
- Same resume appears used for all roles.

## 2. Evidence Quality: 20 Points

Evaluates whether claims are supported by credible proof.

Strong:

- Bullets include real scope, metrics, stakeholders, tools, or outcomes.
- Projects and leadership roles show concrete contribution.
- Evidence connects to target role.

Weak:

- Bullets are responsibility lists.
- Claims are vague.
- No measurable or scoped proof.
- Experience sounds inflated.

## 3. Achievement Writing: 15 Points

Evaluates the strength of bullet construction.

Strong:

- Bullets start with clear action verbs.
- Each bullet explains action and result.
- Language is concise.
- STAR or AIM logic is visible.

Weak:

- Bullets start with "responsible for."
- Sentences are too long.
- Results are missing.
- Actions are unclear.

## 4. Recruiter Readability: 15 Points

Evaluates how quickly a recruiter can understand fit.

Strong:

- Strong evidence appears early.
- Section order is logical.
- Bullets are easy to scan.
- Titles, dates, and organizations are clear.

Weak:

- Dense paragraphs.
- Inconsistent section order.
- Important details hidden.
- Too many minor activities.

## 5. ATS Compatibility: 10 Points

Evaluates parsing and keyword clarity.

Strong:

- Standard headings.
- Relevant keywords.
- Simple text structure.
- Clear dates and labels.

Weak:

- Complex formatting.
- Icons used as labels.
- Keyword stuffing.
- Missing role terms.

## 6. Skills Relevance: 10 Points

Evaluates whether the skills section supports the target role.

Strong:

- Skills are grouped and relevant.
- Tools are credible.
- Skills match job descriptions.

Weak:

- Too many unrelated skills.
- Skills not supported by experience.
- Inflated proficiency.

## 7. Formatting and Polish: 10 Points

Evaluates professional presentation.

Strong:

- Consistent formatting.
- No typos.
- Clean spacing.
- Professional file quality.

Weak:

- Inconsistent fonts or spacing.
- Grammar errors.
- Too decorative.
- Hard to read.

## 8. Integrity and Credibility: 5 Points

Evaluates ethical quality.

Strong:

- Claims are believable.
- Metrics are plausible.
- No fake achievements.
- No misleading titles.

Weak:

- Inflated language.
- Suspicious metrics.
- Fake or unverifiable claims.
- Misleading ownership.

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| 85-100 | Strong resume. Needs targeted polishing. |
| 70-84 | Good foundation. Needs stronger targeting or evidence. |
| 55-69 | Usable but not competitive enough. Requires substantial improvement. |
| 40-54 | Weak resume. Needs restructuring and stronger evidence. |
| Below 40 | Not ready for applications. Build profile and evidence first. |

## Red Flags

Flag immediately:

- Fake internships
- unverifiable metrics
- copied job descriptions
- exaggerated leadership ownership
- irrelevant objective statements
- grammar errors in key sections
- inconsistent dates

## Expected Outputs

- Resume score
- category-level diagnosis
- priority fixes
- rewritten bullets
- ATS recommendations
- role alignment plan
